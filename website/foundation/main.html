FirebirdSQL Foundation ....

The objectives of the FirebirdSQL Foundation are:

  • To support and advance the development of the open source FirebirdSQL relational database engine.
  • To provide the non-commercial infrastructure and mechanisms required to accept and manage funds raised; and disburse such funds to promote and advance the development effort.
  • To encourage cooperation and affiliation with individuals, other non-profit organisations and commercial companies involved in, or planning to become involved in the development, support and promotion of FirebirdSQL software projects and associated products and activities.

Who are we...

Membership

 The FirebirdSQL database engine has a strong following amongst the developer and user communities. The FirebirdSQL Foundation is an organisation of these individuals and companies (of various sizes) working together as part of a non-profit organisation to ensure the continued development of the opensource FirebirdSQL relational database (and related products).

The dues paid by members form play an important part in directly funding development of the FirebirdSQL engine. A list of the current membership is available from here

Legally

Legally the FirebirdSQL Foundation (inc) is a non-profit incoporated Association registered in the state of New South Wales Australia (INC #9878828).

Management

The affairs of the Foundation are managed by the the 'Management Committee' which is elected annually during the annual general meeting, by voting members of the foundation.
The management committee govern in accordance with the rules of the association and they also have the ability to create and delegate tasks to sub-committees as the need arrises.

Meetings within the FF are generally conducted in an online forum

Prior to incorportation the affairs of the Foundation were managed by the FirebirdSQL Founcation Steering Group

When formally registered the foundation will be managed by the: Management Committee which is elected by the members of the association yearly at the annual general meeting to govern according to the rules of the association.
