Procedure for Online Voting

Last updated: 3-Oct-2002

PROCEDURE FOR RAISING QUESTIONS (PROPOSED):

1) - Any list member can post to firebird@123.co.uk a proposal for a formal
question.  the subject line prefixed with a "Q?" to indicate a
proposed question. ie "Q? - do we accept Geoffs Super simple membership
model".
2) - At least two other people have to reply approving the question
before it is approved to put to the steering committee.

3) If approved the originator then re-posts the question again giving it
a "PLEASE VOTE QXX" prefix in the subject line ie: "PLEASE VOTE Q12 - do we accept Geoffs
Super simple mebership model".

4) Everyone the votes by replying to the question with their answer to
that thread.

5) I'll keep track of the results as I have been doing on a web page.


In paralled with steps 2/3 the following can happen:

6) - When a proposed question is posted, (in step 1) everyone in the
group can also post suggestions for modifications to the original question.

7) - The originator of the question is responsible for reviewing any
feedback and modifying the question. If the originator chooses to modify
the question, then he  reposts the modified proposed question as "PROPOSED Q -
   do we want one or two levels of membership".


8) The posing of a modified question requires re-approval (as per step
2) before it is accepted.



ROUGH GUIDES (I've been using):

  • In any vote we get on average 4 people who don't participate, or roughly 15 - 4 = 11 members participating.  So we need to cope with making decisions where varying numbers of members particpate in a vote.  
  • For a result to be valid, it must have been voted for by at least 1/3 of the members with voting rights (ie, 5 votes) and to have received either 2/3 of the votes cast, or votes over 50% of the membership with voting rights(ie, 8 over votes).
This would mean 5/7 (five out of seven votes), 6/8, 6/9, 7/10 or 8 votes if more of us vote (as long as we remain 15 members).
  • If at least two people agree that a vote is particularly important, the rules for voting can be changed to apply to the whole 15 members.  ie to pass a vote must have at least 8 votes (ie over 50% of 15), and at least  For a result to be valid, it must have been voted for by at least 6 votes (ie over 50% of 11), and at least 12 members must have participated in the voting (ie > 3/4 of the 15).
  • Im only posting questions with boolean type answers (ie Yes/No or A/B) currently.
